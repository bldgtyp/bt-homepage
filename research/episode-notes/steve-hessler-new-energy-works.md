**Guest / role:** Steve Hessler — Certified Passive House Consultant & building systems specialist at New Energy Works; visiting educator at McGill University via Holzraum System; 30 yrs timber/CAD-CAM design background

## Summary
Steve Hessler is a CPHC and DFMA (design-for-manufacturing-and-assembly) specialist who panelizes high-performance biogenic (wood-fiber, vapor-open) enclosures at New Energy Works. He argues that you sell Passive House through comfort, health, durability and indoor air quality — not carbon or energy savings, because the energy savings "don't pencil" against the premium. His strongest marketing asset is not finished-project photos but the dramatic, intentionally-art-directed 3D manufacturing models and exploded-assembly graphics his team produces, which consistently grab architects' attention.

## Key Marketing Takeaways
- Lead with comfort, health, durability and indoor air quality — not carbon or energy cost; reduced energy "doesn't pencil for the premium."
- Carbon is the mission but a poor opener: "you're preaching to the choir"; only raise it once you know the audience responds to it.
- Build a "benefits" landing page that lets different people self-select by interest rather than forcing one message.
- Layer information: short paragraph per benefit, with an optional one-click "technical deep dive" for those who want to "go insane."
- Detailed 3D models / design-process visuals win attention and projects, often more than finished photos.
- Start every technical conversation from "points of stasis" — shared agreements (e.g. "we all want to mitigate risk") before introducing the unfamiliar idea.

## Language & Framing
- Don't open with "carbon" or "energy savings" to a general audience — lead with comfort, health, durability, indoor air quality.
- Frame vapor-open assemblies via risk mitigation: imperfect installs trap moisture, "people aren't perfect"; vapor-open lets walls dry rather than "requiring perfect installation."
- Plain-language definition offered: vapor-open "means it can breathe in both directions… no layer in the assembly that is blocking moisture transport."
- Define jargon on the fly (host had to ask what "perm" meant) — assume clients don't know terms.
- "Number one thing should be indoor health and wellness."
- Use connectors / agreement points first because "you can't start a conversation without points of stasis."
- Biogenic-materials selling angle: hygric buffering ("they hydrate buffer… store and release moisture") affects summer latent load and "the feeling in the house," plus durability from no trapped moisture.

## Format & Channel
- A dedicated benefits landing page to "just send people to" — eight benefits, each expandable from a paragraph to a deep technical dive on click.
- Print/PDF brochures featuring CAD-work model graphics that architects react to viscerally.
- Exportable interactive 3D "nugget" viewer files (a cropped clone of any region of the model) sent to architects, engineers, and clients.
- Conference teaching: PhiusCon sessions/workshops, a half-day Greenbuild lecture (~300 attendees) that directly generated the McGill university relationship.
- Website: newenergyworks.com (timber + enclosure portfolio) and holzraumsystem.com (education + energy-modeling consulting).

## Scope of Work & Offerings
- Panelized high-performance biogenic enclosures (wood-fiber insulation, vapor-open walls/roofs) manufactured in-house; two factory wall types brought back from Steico Germany tour.
- Timber framing (40 yrs) and mass timber / CLT — first all-CLT building in NY State; currently a 4-story CLT building in Syracuse.
- Holzraum's "Single Integrated Manufacturing Model" (IDFMA) service: deep modeling with load-path, MEP clash detection, every stud and fastener (5,000+ fasteners, up to 20,000 parts).
- Ilka Cassidy (Holzraum) offers standalone energy-modeling / CPHC consulting (WUFI) and cost-benefit analysis.
- Education: McGill 3-year curriculum teaching DFMA + Passive House to M.Arch students using Cadwork.
- Bundled partnership model: homeowner enters via Holzraum → New Energy Works does design + timber + enclosure, Ilka does PH certification/energy model.

## What Works
- Art-directing model graphics "like a photographer" — drama, tuned perspective, good shadowing — makes M.Arch student and firm output stand out.
- Showing the design/modeling process and "showing your work," not just the finished building.
- Interactive viewer files ("nuggets"), layer transparencies, and "context vs focus" highlighting (critical parts in white contextual background) to communicate complex assemblies.
- Conference lectures as lead-gen (Greenbuild talk → McGill 3-year contract).
- Self-select benefits page so each prospect follows the touchpoint that resonates with them.

## What Fails
- Leading with carbon/energy savings to a general audience — flops because it doesn't pencil and preaches to the choir.
- Assuming clients know terms (perm, vapor-open) — leaving jargon undefined loses people.
- Note: some clients perceive heavy 3D modeling as "Steve's just wasting our time and showing off" — not everyone values the visuals.

## What Clients/People Like
- Comfort, health, indoor air quality, and building longevity ("no trapped moisture").
- "A lot of them would rather hear about the longevity of the building… and indoor air quality."
- Architects react strongly to the model graphics: "Oh my God, that's such a great graphic."
- Completed project photos remain "the best sale."
- Power of an energy modeler who "can just run a WUFI model and tell you where your cost benefit is — it's magic."

## What Clients/People Dislike / Objections
- Not all clients are bought into spending extra for Passive House: "they're not all bought in."
- Energy-cost savings alone don't justify the premium.
- Some clients see deep modeling as time-wasting / showing off.
- Carbon messaging lands flat with audiences who already agree (or don't care).

## Audience & Positioning
- Target architects to influence early design and win collaboration ("trying to get the message out about what we're doing").
- Segment by climate: walls targeted at heating-dominated zones 4A/5A/6A (and 4B/5B/6B).
- Position around manufacturing-integrated design: "more architects should be more connected with manufacturing" (the European model where architects feed logic to factories).
- Differentiate on biogenic/vapor-open + DFMA depth, not generic Passive House.
- Partner positioning: pair DFMA/enclosure expertise with a strong PH energy modeler (Ilka) for full-service credibility.
