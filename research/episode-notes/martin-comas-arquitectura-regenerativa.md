# 05 — Martín Comas (Arquitectura Regenerativa)
**Listen:** https://marketingpassivhaus.com/episodes/martin-comas-arquitectura-regenerativa
**Transcript:** research/transcripts/martin-comas-arquitectura-regenerativa.md
**Guest / role:** Martín Comas — founder & architect, Arquitectura Regenerativa (Uruguay); Uruguay referent for the Latin American Passive House Institute

## Summary
Martín Comas builds low-cost, fast, carbon-negative, near-Passive-House homes in Uruguay using straw bale, wood frame, wood chips and cellulose, aiming to make Passive House accessible to "normal people, not only rich people." His marketing thesis: lead with health and low energy bills (the "Trojan horse"), never with "Passive House" or natural materials, and keep both the building method and the messaging radically simple. His "car-tool housing" concept — a whole house buildable with tools that fit on a car's front seat — is a coined phrase designed to make self-building feel achievable.

## Key Marketing Takeaways
- With clients, never open with "Passive House" — open with health and low energy bills, then reveal the method later.
- Coin a simple, sticky phrase that compresses your whole value proposition ("car-tool housing").
- Reframe Passive House as cheaper and faster, not more expensive — actively reject the "PH costs more" narrative.
- The continuous-fresh-air / health benefit is the "tipping point": once clients grasp it, "they don't want to go back."
- Position PH as accessible to ordinary people, not a luxury for the rich.
- A vivid concrete image (tools sitting on a car seat) makes the offer feel real and attainable.

## Language & Framing
- Lead word for clients = "health" and "low energy bills," not "Passive House": "you don't have to talk to them to tell them about passive house. You have to tell them about health, and then you go, you have them."
- Calls the health / low-bills angle the "Trojan horses" — the entry point before discussing natural materials.
- "Car-tool housing" — coined phrase: a house built with only the few tools that fit in a car.
- "Push the limit" / "dreamer" — personal brand framing beyond just "architect."
- Brand tagline: "pollinating sovereignty and empowering communities."
- "Sovereignty" — food, water, energy independence as emotional payoff for clients.
- Frames PH as "the essential one," going "to the basics… all the way to the best," and independent ("not tied to anything or to anybody").
- "Wet" vs "dry" construction as a plain-language distinction (avoid wet construction that needs weeks to dry / weather delays).

## Scope of Work & Offerings
- Self-build / self-building Passive-House concept: empowering owners to build their own home fast and cheap, avoiding mortgages and banks.
- Carbon-negative / carbon-neutral homes (e.g., Casa AYA straw-bale, "almost passive house").
- Shifted from "wet" (masonry) to "dry" construction: wood frame, wood chips, cellulose insulation.
- ~50 m² home buildable by two people in ~1.5 months, near-PH and carbon-neutral.
- Insulation tuned to climate (6 inches vs 4) but same core method.
- Knowledge-sharing / regional network-building via the Latin American Passive House Institute (19+ countries).

## What Works
- Leading client conversations with health and energy-bill savings.
- Real lived proof: a family living two winters with no fireplace/heating, no rent, low bills, now planting a food forest.
- Delivering on (and beating) the timeline promise — quoted 3 months, built in 2.
- Using locally available natural materials available NOW (straw, cellulose, wood chips) instead of waiting.
- A memorable image/phrase that simplifies a complex offer.

## What Fails
- Pitching architects first — they're entrenched ("I always been doing this way") and harder to convince than clients.
- Opening with "natural materials" or technical detail before establishing the health/cost hook.
- Waiting for "perfect" trendy materials (hempcrete, mycelium) that aren't actually available — "that moment will never come."
- Off-site/prefab modular at scale hits space, transport, slope, tree, and weather limits; "not as scalable as you can think."
- Wet construction: weather delays, slow drying, needs cranes and large crews.

## What Clients/People Like
- Health and continuously fresh air (renewed every ~3 hours) — the decisive selling point.
- Lower energy bills and no rent/mortgage.
- Speed and low cost of construction.
- Sense of sovereignty/empowerment — building their own future, food forests, independence.
- The simplicity and achievability of the self-build approach.

## What Clients/People Dislike / Objections
- Disbelief that Passive House can be cheaper — "they don't believe me once I tell them it's going to be cheaper."
- First-time builders have nothing to compare against, so they must simply trust the builder.
- Architects resist: "This is so strange… I always been doing this way."
- Skepticism overcome only via a friend's prior experience or a different (health) angle.

## Audience & Positioning
- Target end-clients / homeowners (especially younger generations, self-builders), NOT fellow architects.
- Position PH as accessible, affordable, fast, healthy and sovereignty-granting — for "normal people, not only rich people."
- Position the firm/founder as a regional connector and open-source sharer of knowledge.

## Flags for BLDGTYP Homepage
- Lead homepage messaging with comfort/health and lower operating costs before the term "Passive House."
- Counter the "PH is expensive / for the rich" objection head-on with proof points (cost, speed).
- Use one compressed, memorable phrase or image to convey the value proposition.
- Feature lived-experience case studies (occupant testimony: comfort, no heating, low bills) as primary proof.
- Consider documenting build process on video (YouTube) to make the work tangible and trustworthy.
- Audience targeting note: homeowners respond to experience/benefits; technically-entrenched professionals resist — tailor pages accordingly.
