**Guest / role:** John Loercher — CPHC / owner-consultant at Northeast Projects, professor & director of Building Science program at RPI, Phius CPHC trainer (since 2017)

## Summary
A wide-ranging, mostly biographical conversation with a CPHC consultant and Phius trainer working across the Northeast and now Colorado. The strongest marketing signal is experiential selling: letting prospects physically spend a night in a certified Passive House (via an Airbnb) so they feel the comfort/air-quality difference, and leading with health/comfort/"experience" rather than energy specs. Also notable: external shock events (Marshall Fire) and utility incentives (MA, Denver) are creating organic demand, and a "50 projects" retrospective campaign on Instagram as ongoing content.

## Key Marketing Takeaways
- Sell the *experience* of the building, not the certification — "that's what it all comes down to, is that experience."
- Get prospects to physically stay overnight in a real certified Passive House before they commit ("Go stay at Dan Levy's house").
- Health and comfort (feeling "safe in your space," indoor air quality) are the most emotionally resonant hooks, especially for families.
- Quantifiability is a selling point: Passive House removes subjectivity by giving measurable targets you can prove you hit.
- External events (wildfires) and utility incentives convert people who never previously cared about airtightness into interested buyers.

## Language & Framing
- Lead with "comfortable," "healthy," "high-quality interiors," "feel safe in your space," "amazing indoor air quality" — emotional/experiential, not technical.
- "Drank the Kool-Aid" / "value-aligned" — frames PH as an identity/values alignment, not just a spec.
- "Catalyzing constraints" / "puzzle" — frame PH design as satisfying intellectual problem-solving ("if you're into puzzles and you're an architect, you'll love passive house").
- "Figure out what your objectives are" — define measurable objectives up front so you're "working towards something you feel good about"; clients latch onto this.
- "Meet or aspire to" the standard — building science benefits (longer-lasting, healthier, more comfortable) apply with or without certification, a non-pejorative framing for non-certified work.

## Format & Channel
- Instagram is where the guest is "most active right now" (@ne_projects) — primary discovery/connection channel.
- Website (ne-projects.com) as the formal presence.
- A retrospective campaign: sharing all 50 certified projects one by one, collecting images and recapping each — a built-in content series from existing portfolio.
- Airbnb listing of a certified unit as a demonstration/lead-conversion tool.

## Scope of Work & Offerings
- CPHC / Passive House consulting (Phius), 50 design certifications to date, expanding from the Northeast into Colorado/Denver (6 certified projects with a partner firm).
- Single-family and second-home work; affordable housing referenced as a high-impact PH application.
- Expertise applies to non-certified work too — phased retrofits where certification is "impossible" but thermal-bridge reduction, hygrothermal detailing, etc. still deliver value.
- Phius CPHC training (incl. business-of-consulting modules: liabilities, timing, scheduling, coordination with consultants).

## What Works
- Inviting prospective clients to stay a night in a certified Passive House Airbnb to experience it firsthand.
- Leveraging a regional PH peer group to funnel prospects to a shared demonstration property.
- Riding utility incentive programs (Massachusetts, Denver-area) that make single-family certification financially attractive.
- Turning a portfolio milestone (50th certification) into an ongoing social-media content campaign.

## What Fails
_(none)_

## What Clients/People Like
- Comfort and the felt "experience" of living in the building.
- Indoor air quality and feeling safe at home — decisive for a health-sensitive (immunocompromised) family who stayed months because it was one of the only buildings they could safely live in.
- Clear, measurable objectives / removal of subjectivity.
- Value alignment — doing work that matches their values (people, incl. the guest, took pay cuts to do PH work).

## What Clients/People Dislike / Objections
- Fire-related sensitivities can override standard building-science solutions — a wildfire-affected client rejected a vented roof (the "no-brainer" fix) because anything fire-related is a sensitive subject, forcing a costlier unvented detail.
- Resistance to changing established ways of working (professionals retraining find it "frustrating" to relearn a familiar task a new way).

## Audience & Positioning
- Position health/comfort/safety first; energy efficiency second (the latter is increasingly forced by code anyway).
- Families with health concerns are a high-conviction audience.
- Value-aligned builders/contractors are easier partners — some construction companies are "really into it" and aligned with the low-energy movement.
- Affordable housing as both impact story and market (renters/lower-wealth occupants have least control, so delivering PH there matters most).
- Regions with incentives + recent disasters (Denver post-Marshall Fire) are ripe expansion markets.

## Flags for BLDGTYP Homepage
- Lead homepage messaging with comfort / healthy / clean-air / "feel safe in your space" language; keep energy/technical specs secondary.
- Consider an experiential proof element — testimonial or invitation to experience a finished PH (overnight stay / open house) as the conversion mechanism.
- A portfolio-milestone retrospective ("50 projects") is a credible, content-rich format; BLDGTYP could surface its own project count / case-study series.
- Explicitly state that building-science expertise applies to non-certified and phased-retrofit work, not only full certification — broadens the addressable audience.
- Call out incentive programs (utility, state) as a reason-to-act for single-family clients.
- A health/family case study (immunocompromised occupant finding safety in a PH) is a powerful, emotionally resonant homepage story type.
