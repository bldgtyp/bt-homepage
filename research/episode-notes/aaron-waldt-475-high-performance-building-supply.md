**Guest / role:** Aaron Waldt — Product Consultant for Eastern Canada (Ontario, Quebec, Maritimes), 475 High Performance Building Supply

## Summary
Aaron Waldt sells the Pro Clima airtightness system and other PH-certified components for 475, a supplier that positions itself as "a knowledge resource company that sells tape." His marketing is education-first: roughly 50% architect-office presentations and 50% on-site builder training, with content driven by listening for recurring questions. He describes the market maturing from "why" (selling the concept) to "how" (trickier airtightness/control-layer detailing), and stresses making the value proposition clear and distinct for each party — homeowner, architect, builder.

## Key Marketing Takeaways
- Sell the thing by teaching how/why it works — to sell tape you must explain envelope physics, which simultaneously sells the whole concept of high-performance building.
- Pair products with knowledge resources from day one; education is the differentiator, not just the materials.
- Mine recurring questions from site visits/meetings to decide which workshops and content to produce — be responsive to the market, even regionally specific feedback.
- Track market maturity and move your messaging upstream from "why" (vapor control basics) to "how" (advanced airtightness/control-layer detailing).
- Make the takeaway explicit for each stakeholder: homeowner gets energy/health/durability, builder gets fewer callbacks and saved time/money.
- Let big third-party voices (Matt Risinger, NS Builders YouTube) and policymakers/code do de-risking work for you — once they talk about a product it becomes common knowledge.
- Certifications double as marketing seals/trust signals on a website, beyond their technical function.

## Language & Framing
- Frame the four control layers explicitly: water, air, vapor, thermal — and teach the distinctions ("Is it a vapor barrier? a vapor control layer? a vapor variable?").
- "Treat the house as a system" — a recurring framing tied to the engineering mindset.
- Compare a house to a device (iPad): every homeowner should get a "Passive House user's manual" / operating guide.
- Use the "food ingredient list" / "candy wrapper label" metaphor to explain Declare labels (material transparency).
- Lead with health, comfort, durability, longevity — not just energy efficiency; tap the "health and wellness movement in architecture."
- Translate construction jargon for builders (watertight vs weathertight) — an entire seminar is built around demystifying terminology.
- The "475" name itself is a Passive House Easter egg (4.75 kBTU/sf/yr heating load) — embedded brand storytelling.

## Format & Channel
- Monthly online seminars ("Building Blocks" presentations) on one specific topic each.
- Seminars posted to YouTube in long form immediately after the live presentation.
- In-person architect-office presentations (~50% of time) and hands-on on-site builder trainings (~50%).
- Instagram as the primary social channel (lots of video + photo); builders skew Instagram.
- LinkedIn increasingly used because architects favor it — match channel to audience.
- Website as the central hub for all information (475.supply).
- Big-creator YouTube channels (Matt Risinger, NS Builders) as third-party amplification reaching hundreds of thousands of builders.

## Scope of Work & Offerings
- Supplier of Pro Clima airtightness system (tapes, smart vapor-variable membranes like Intello), Lamilux skylights, Brink ventilation (HRV/ERV), Gutex woodfiber — all from Germany/Netherlands.
- Free material takeoffs and plan review: "send me a set of the drawings" and Aaron recommends what materials go where.
- Brink team designs the entire ventilation system from drawings, then supports plug-and-play install.
- Builder training as a core service: fastest/most efficient air-sealing methods, window-install detailing tuned to each window manufacturer's taping requirements.
- Retrofit assembly consulting (tying new control layers into existing ones) — more variables, more demand.
- Regional rep model: a knowledgeable person assigned to nearly every state/province for local support.

## What Works
- Education tied directly to products; being a genuine "knowledge resource."
- Listening for recurring question themes and turning them into targeted trainings/seminars.
- Tailoring window-install training to the specific window manufacturer on each site.
- Regional reps giving climate- and vernacular-specific advice.
- Free takeoffs/plan reviews as a value-add that builds the relationship.
- Riding third-party credibility (YouTubers, building code, Passive House Canada) to normalize products.
- Monthly single-topic seminars with consistently good turnout, archived on YouTube.

## What Clients/People Like
- Builders like using good, reliable materials they can trust not to fail/leak down the road — pride in craft.
- Builders value avoiding remedial callback visits (cracked drywall, leaks) — durability saves them time and money.
- Homeowners increasingly care what's actually in their walls (health, off-gassing/VOCs, embodied carbon).
- Architects increasingly require EPDs and low-embodied-carbon materials.
- People value the service level from 475 (takeoffs, recommendations, system design).
- Passive House certification read as a proxy for quality even on non-certified projects (partially).

## What Clients/People Dislike / Objections
- "Why are we using this?" — skepticism about a specified product the builder doesn't understand (diminishing as membranes become commonplace).
- Standards disconnect: PH/European certification alone isn't enough; North American buyers/inspectors require ASTM/CanULC testing — a credibility hurdle 475 must clear with North American test data.
- Prioritization problem: clients lavish budget on kitchens/finishes while walls/roofs hit only code minimum.

## Audience & Positioning
- Three audiences served together: homeowners, architects, builders — with a distinct value message for each.
- 475 positions itself as an advocate and knowledge resource, not merely a supplier ("knowledge resource company that sells tape").
- Position the firm as the people who help projects hit timeline and budget through efficient use of the system.
- Lean into the health/wellness + durability + energy narrative, not energy alone.
