# 21 — Valentine Gomez (Gomex Engineering)
**Listen:** https://marketingpassivhaus.com/episodes/valentine-gomez-gomex-engineering
**Transcript:** research/transcripts/valentine-gomez-gomex-engineering.md
**Guest / role:** Valentine Gomez — engineer, Certified Passive House Designer, PH Tradesperson, PH Construction Verifier, Living Future accredited; founder of Gomex Engineering (Prince Edward Island, Canada)

## Summary
Valentine Gomez is a former petroleum engineer turned Certified Passive House Designer, the first dedicated PH designer on Prince Edward Island, who bundles passive house, low-embodied-carbon materials, and Red List-free healthy materials into a single "trifecta" offering. He frames the work not as "green building" but as simply "good buildings," and uses his own gut-renovation as a proof-of-concept demonstrating air-tightness (0.57 ACH50), comfort, and grid resilience. Much of the episode is about marketing PH to the trades (education, performance feedback, blower-door theatre) and reframing the economics away from naive simple-payback. He argues PH is "not rocket science," just a better-built house requiring attention to detail.

## Key Marketing Takeaways
- Sell "good buildings," not "green buildings" — nobody will say "I don't want a good building," but many will balk at "green" (= expensive connotation).
- Bundle PH + low-embodied-carbon + healthy/Red List-free materials as a single "trifecta" full-service offering; they reinforce each other naturally.
- Use your own home/renovation as a living showcase ("exemplification of what Gomex does").
- Reframe economics: throw out simple payback; factor in 13-year (not 5-year) average ownership, escalating energy rates, and resilience.
- Market PH to the trades, not just homeowners — once a builder sees blower-door proof they "can't unlearn passive house."
- Onboard clients with an explicit priority matrix / design criteria up front to drive decisions and avoid back-and-forth.

## Language & Framing
- "He doesn't sell people green buildings anymore. He just sells people good buildings" — hard to refuse a "good building," easy to refuse a "green building."
- "Green buildings are expensive" is the connotation to avoid/counter.
- "Passive house is nothing special. It's just a better built house" (attrib. Michael Ingui).
- "Passive house is not rocket science."
- "Setting up your materials for success" — framing of correct install sequencing.
- "Belts and suspenders approach" for PH redundancy/robustness.
- "The red door of truth" — colloquial, dramatized name for the blower-door test.
- "You can't unlearn passive house" — once shown, builders won't go back.
- "Passive house adjacent" (Passive House Canada term) for non-certified but better-than-code builds.
- Comfort framed technically: no temperature stratification, <4.2 °C head-to-feet differential as the threshold of perceptible discomfort.
- The "little girl in shorts/t-shirt playing by the window in winter" image as a comfort icon.

## Format & Channel
- Personal renovation as a demonstration project / case study.
- Blower-door + theatrical fog machine test as a visible, dramatic performance demo (smoke leaks visible along a roof seam).
- Becoming a PH Tradesperson course instructor to close the local trades education/skills gap.
- iPhone-launch-line analogy to dramatize the public's indifference to building tech ("people will wait in line for the latest iPhone but shrug at the latest building tech").
- Website www.gomex-engineering.com as the contact/find-me channel (lightweight online presence).

## Scope of Work & Offerings
- "Trifecta" full service: PH design + low-embodied-carbon material selection + healthy/Red List-free material specification.
- PH Construction Verifier services — on-site field verification, add-on to PH design, mostly for larger commercial work.
- Building commissioning.
- GC / construction management (self-GC'd own renovation; managed schedule, budget, ordering, trades).
- Recommends a multi-stage blower-door regimen: post-MEP/pre-drywall test plus a final pre-handover test, feeding the final number into the energy model.
- Aspires to teach the PH Tradesperson course locally (instructor role).

## What Works
- Priority matrix collected at onboarding — resolves PH-vs-embodied-carbon conflicts and makes design decisions defensible to clients.
- On-site daily presence enabling real-time QC during install.
- Blower-door + fog-machine demo gives builders tangible performance feedback they get a "thrill" out of.
- Explaining the *why* of each detail to trades (e.g., why not to perforate the air barrier) wins them over so they adopt principles on future jobs.
- Quality framing wins: level/square/plumb framing meant cabinets and stone countertops dropped in with zero adjustment — a tangible craftsmanship selling point.
- Resilience story: house held above 17.9 °C with no heat from May to Nov 11; rolling-blackout warnings make resilience newly relevant.

## What Fails
- Simple-payback math fails PH — ignores escalating energy prices and real ownership duration; should be discarded as the framing.
- The "we'll only be here 5 years, no payback" mindset (real avg is 13 years) holds PH back.
- Trades doing it "the way I was always taught" — e.g., hammer-tack instead of cap stapler (voids Tyvek warranty), siding nails Swiss-cheesing the air/weather barrier.
- Code on PEI requires blower-door testing but it's not enforced — performance verification doesn't happen unless the homeowner pushes for it.

## What Clients/People Like
- Comfort: no temperature stratification, even temps everywhere even at -26 °C.
- Resilience against rolling blackouts / cold snaps.
- The performance "game" — seeing how long the house coasts without heat.
- Trades take pride when their work produces measurable, superior results.
- Healthy indoor air quality and chemical-free materials (good for occupant, installer, and factory worker — "all the way up the life cycle").

## What Clients/People Dislike / Objections
- "Green = expensive" connotation.
- Payback skepticism: "you want me to spend X on a better ERV or windows — where's the payback in 5 years?"
- Trades resistance to new methods ("threw a bit of a fuss" when told not to cut the membrane).
- Certification cost — certification is usually dictated by finances; PH-adjacent is an acceptable lower-cost path.

## Audience & Positioning
- Position as the local PH expert/first-mover and advocate ("standing on my soapbox, beating the drum").
- Wants MORE competitors/designers locally — more advocates grow the market; one person can't move it alone.
- Strong economic case for institutional/municipal clients: buildings meant to last 100 years, municipalities pay ~33¢ on the dollar (province + feds cover the rest) but 100% of operating costs — so not building PH is fiscally negligent.
- Target the "other stakeholder" (e.g., the non-nerdy spouse) via comfort/resilience/health, not building science.

## Flags for BLDGTYP Homepage
- Lead with "good / better-built / healthy / comfortable buildings," not "green" — reduces the expensive-connotation barrier.
- Present a bundled offering (PH + embodied carbon + healthy/Red List materials) as integrated value, not separate add-ons.
- Use measured air-tightness numbers (e.g., before/after ACH50) as concrete proof points / case-study content.
- Feature resilience (grid blackouts, coasting without heat) and comfort (no stratification, even temps) as homeowner-facing benefits distinct from the building-science story.
- Offer/visualize an explicit client priority-matrix onboarding step as a differentiator and trust-builder.
- Counter simple-payback objections with content on real ownership duration (13 yrs) + rising energy costs + lifecycle/operational savings; especially compelling for institutional/municipal clients.
- List verification/commissioning (blower-door testing, construction verification) as named services — buyers may not know to ask for them.
