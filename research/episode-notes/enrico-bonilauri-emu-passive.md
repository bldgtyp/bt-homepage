**Guest / role:** Enrico Bonilauri — architect, trainer, Passive House certifier, CEO of Emu Passive

## Summary
Bonilauri co-founded Emu as an Italian architecture practice ~15 years ago, then pivoted entirely to hands-on Passive House design-build training after moving to Colorado (~2016), concluding that education propagates ideas with more durable impact than designing individual buildings. He treats education/training/lobbying as marketing — running free topical research presentations as a top-of-funnel for the Passive House concept overall. Key lessons: reframe building science around the buyer's actual concern (comfort/health/durability, not energy or even ROI), tailor content sharply by platform (LinkedIn = the "why"/data; Instagram = the "how"/visuals), and lean on word-of-mouth plus utility/state reimbursement deals to scale.

## Key Marketing Takeaways
- Reframe the pitch to the audience's real concern: not energy, not even payback — often comfort, health, durability ("It's not about energy").
- Convert abstract technical metrics into felt outcomes the buyer understands (U-value → interior surface temperature → "you're gonna be cold vs. not cold").
- Treat free, advanced, topic-specific presentations as a funnel for the Passive House concept itself, not lead-gen for a single service.
- Don't do "Passive House 101" — there's plenty of that already; differentiate with advanced, climate-/project-specific research.
- Match content to platform: same post performs wildly differently on LinkedIn vs Instagram.
- Position against building code: show how bad code-built is (air quality, comfort, durability) to give clients language to "do better even where legislation isn't requiring it yet."
- Pursue institutional reimbursement (utilities, states, cities) — back it with hard outcome data from past trainees to prove a decarbonization path.

## Language & Framing
- Lead with "comfortable," "healthy," "durable" — not "energy efficient" and not U-values, when talking to non-technical buyers.
- A code-minimum window = "you're gonna be cold"; a Passive House window = "you're gonna be not cold." Visceral, not numeric.
- Translate U-value into interior surface temperature in degrees so a layperson grasps it; once she understood, "there was no question."
- "More adult / mature / holistic / engineering-mature approach to building science" — repeated framing for Passive House's performance-based rigor vs checkbox certifications.
- On thermal bridges: it's not "is there a thermal bridge yes/no" — it's "how bad" and "which ones do we choose to mitigate"; "wood frame thermal bridging, who cares?" vs a steel beam = "a vein coming down."
- "Design certified" Passive House "doesn't mean anything... do people live in the drawing?" — frame value around built/lived performance, not drawings.
- Meet ROI-focused clients (e.g. a CPA) on their terms first, but redirect: money matters "less so than we would expect."

## Format & Channel
- LinkedIn = technical/data/graphs, design + engineering audience, the "why"; data-heavy posts succeed here.
- Instagram = visual, construction videos, taping/air-sealing details, the "how"; data posts get "crickets."
- Run on-demand + in-person hybrid training; introduced hybrid to cut required in-person time from 4.5 to 2.5 days.
- Free live presentations with a public calendar on emupassive.com (windows, air quality, thermal comfort, retrofits, warm-climate PH).
- YouTube for thermal-bridge analysis content (15 yrs of modeling) — searchable, builds authority.
- Dropped Twitter (~10-15 yrs ago) and doesn't use TikTok; focuses on LinkedIn + Instagram.

## Scope of Work & Offerings
- Core business: integrated Passive House design-build training bringing builders and architects together to talk to each other.
- Hands-on builder training: 2 days theory + 2 days hands-on + half-day exam (4.5 days); hybrid option moves first 2 days on-demand.
- Public bootcamps (individuals or small teams) and private booked sessions for larger corporate teams (10-12+).
- Fully-online option exists but is positioned as "less enriching" — you can't learn air-sealing from a PowerPoint.
- Still does a little Passive House consulting, used partly to generate climate-/project-specific research that feeds the presentations.
- Goal: 5,000 builders trained by 2030 (passed 1,000; ~250 expected this year).

## What Works
- Reframing technical value into the client's language closed the Gunnison window sale with no further objection.
- Free topical research presentations attract topic-driven audiences (windows → architects/developers; construction defects → builders).
- Word-of-mouth drives the client base; Instagram "refreshes the memory" and prompts referrals.
- LinkedIn builds technical validity/credibility with the design-engineering crowd.
- Utility reimbursement (Xcel in Colorado reimburses 90% of training fees) — secured via ongoing relationship-building, not a cold ask.
- Workforce-development funding from states/cities/utilities is far more available than 10 years ago.

## What Fails
- Posting data/numbers/graphs on Instagram — "people don't care," "that's not what they're looking for."
- Leading a ROI-minded client with U-values and energy framing — "I was framing the topic in a completely wrong way."
- Selling "design certified" status as an end in itself — meaningless until built.
- Overlong in-person commitment (4.5 days) is the #1 objection from working professionals/employers.

## What Clients/People Like
- Comfort, health, durability, low energy bills, no mold/condensation — the lived outcomes.
- Hybrid/flexible format that reduces time off work.
- Hands-on, in-person practice trying real products and details to "see what works."
- Reimbursement/funding that lowers the cost barrier.

## What Clients/People Dislike / Objections
- Time cost of training (4.5 days) — hardest for working pros/employers to commit.
- Skepticism that a "brand new window" could still be cold — expectation mismatch to overcome.
- ROI/payback framing dominates for finance-minded buyers until reframed around comfort.

## Audience & Positioning
- Trainees: ~half builders (GCs, PMs, site supervisors), ~third architects/designers, rest product reps, homeowners, developers.
- Position Emu as the main hands-on Passive House training provider in the US; mission "to bring the most advanced building science to mainstream construction."
- Position Passive House as performance-based and "mature" — answers many needs (health, comfort, durability, decarbonization) where checkbox systems (LEED, BS+B) lose sight of real performance.
- Acknowledge PH's limits honestly (doesn't cover transportation, embodied carbon) but note other goals can be woven in.
