# Marketing Passive House — Master Rules & Best Practices

*Distilled from 38 episodes of the "Marketing Passivhaus" podcast (host: James Turner). Each rule is cited to the guest(s) who voiced it, using `[Guest — slug]`. Rules echoed by many guests are flagged **HIGH CONSENSUS**.*

---

## How to use this

This is a field guide, not a textbook. Read the **Top 10 Rules** first — they are the load-bearing principles almost every guest converged on. Then mine the thematic sections for specific language, tactics, and objection-handling you can lift directly into copy, sales conversations, and content. Citations let you trace any rule back to the practitioner who earned it, so you can weigh a lone opinion against a 20-person consensus. When two guests disagree, see **Notable Tensions**. This document deliberately captures the *rules*; applying them to any specific page or pitch is a separate step.

## Executive summary — the biggest cross-cutting themes

The single most-repeated lesson across the entire series: **do not lead with "Passive House," energy, carbon, or technical metrics.** People buy on emotion and justify with logic, so you lead with the felt, universal, non-arguable human benefits — **comfort, health/clean air, quiet, durability, resilience, and safety** — and let energy efficiency "come along for the ride" as proof, not pitch. The second great theme is **audience segmentation**: the same building requires completely different messaging for homeowners (lived experience), architects (design + integration), builders/trades (craft, fewer callbacks, de-risking), developers (ROI, operating cost, risk management), and policymakers (grid, codes, affordability). Third, **experience beats explanation** — getting a prospect physically inside a finished Passive House (tour, overnight Airbnb stay) converts better than any spec sheet, because the benefits are invisible in a photo. Fourth, **cost is the universal objection**, and the winning moves are reframing the "premium" (a learning curve, a rounding error, 2–3% that evaporates against day-one operating savings) and proving it with hard data. Fifth, **education and measured-performance data ARE the marketing** — give value first, show predicted-vs-actual numbers, and position third-party certification as durability insurance that de-risks the project.

## Top 10 Rules (ranked by signal strength)

**1. Lead with comfort, health, and the lived experience — never with energy, carbon, or "Passive House." HIGH CONSENSUS (~30+ of 38 guests).**
Energy and GHG are imperceptible and weak motivators; comfort/health/safety/quiet are universal and unarguable. Solve for those and "the energy comes along for the ride." [Kristof Irwin — kristof-irwin-positive-energy] [Jacob Deva Racusin — jacob-deva-racusin-new-frameworks] [Lloyd Alter — lloyd-alter-carbon-upfront] [Wolfgang Feist — dr-wolfgang-feist-passive-house-institute] [Josh Salinger — josh-salinger-birdsmouth-design-build] [Beth Campbell — beth-campbell-passive-house-massachusetts] [Enrico Bonilauri — enrico-bonilauri-emu-passive] [Zack Semke — zack-semke-passive-house-accelerator] [Elrond Burrell — elrond-burrell-via-architecture] [Graham Irwin — graham-irwin-essential-habitat-architecture] and many more.

**2. People decide on emotion and justify with logic — sell to emotion. HIGH CONSENSUS.**
"People don't buy for logical reasons, they buy for emotional reasons" (Ziglar via Alter). PH marketing has historically over-indexed on rational/technical argument to its detriment. [Lloyd Alter — lloyd-alter-carbon-upfront] [Graham Irwin — graham-irwin-essential-habitat-architecture] [Alexis Jarvis — alexis-jarvis-sidecar-pr] [Kristof Irwin — kristof-irwin-positive-energy].

**3. Segment the message by audience — homeowner ≠ architect ≠ builder ≠ developer ≠ policymaker. HIGH CONSENSUS (~15+ guests).**
Same product, totally different message and call-to-action per segment; "if you talk to all of them the same way, you're not gonna get anywhere." [Mariana Pickering — mariana-pickering-b-public-prefab] [Adam White — adam-white-intelligent-membranes] [Aaron Waldt — aaron-waldt-475-high-performance-building-supply] [Michael Quast — michael-quast-passive-house-canada] [Beth Eckenrode — beth-eckenrode-auros-group] [Brian Pearson — brian-pearson-studio-pear] [Shefali Sanghvi — shefali-sanghvi-dattner-architects].

**4. Get people physically inside a Passive House — experience beats explanation. HIGH CONSENSUS (~12 guests).**
Tours, overnight Airbnb stays, demo units, open houses. The benefit is felt, not seen; "the way to market passive houses is to put people in them, over and over, like a theme park." [Wolfgang Feist — dr-wolfgang-feist-passive-house-institute] [Graham Irwin — graham-irwin-essential-habitat-architecture] [John Loercher — john-loercher-northeast-projects] [Trey Farmer — trey-farmer-forge-craft-architecture-design] [Tessa Bradley — tessa-bradley-artisans-group] [Lorrie Rand — lorrie-rand-habit-studio] [Jacob Deva Racusin — jacob-deva-racusin-new-frameworks] [Brian Pearson — brian-pearson-studio-pear] [Bronwyn Barry — bronwyn-barry-passive-house-bb].

**5. Reframe the cost objection — the premium is a myth, a learning curve, or a rounding error. HIGH CONSENSUS (~15 guests).**
Cost is the dominant objection everywhere. Counter with hard data (2–3% multifamily premium), lifetime/compounding savings, and "the premium evaporates against day-one operating savings." [Alexander Gard-Murray — alexander-gard-murray-passive-house-massachusetts] [Beth Eckenrode — beth-eckenrode-auros-group] ("not a premium, a learning curve") [Hans Breaux — hans-breaux-project-co-op] [Katrina Belle — katrina-belle-maine-passive-house] [Trey Farmer — trey-farmer-forge-craft-architecture-design] [Wolfgang Feist — dr-wolfgang-feist-passive-house-institute] [Michael Quast — michael-quast-passive-house-canada].

**6. The name "Passive House" is a liability — don't lead with it. HIGH CONSENSUS (~12 guests).**
"Passive" reads as weak/negative; people confuse it with passive solar; it's "neither of the things in its name." Drop benefits first, soft-reveal the label later. [Lloyd Alter — lloyd-alter-carbon-upfront] [Zack Semke — zack-semke-passive-house-accelerator] [Beth Eckenrode — beth-eckenrode-auros-group] [Beth Campbell — beth-campbell-passive-house-massachusetts] [Lorrie Rand — lorrie-rand-habit-studio] [Rachel Rennard & Mike Bratina — rachel-rennard-mike-bratina-clay-brook-passive-house] [Mariana Pickering — mariana-pickering-b-public-prefab].

**7. Prove it with measured performance data — predicted ≈ actual. HIGH CONSENSUS.**
Post-occupancy monitoring and measured-vs-modeled results beat certification logos for client confidence and close the "performance gap" story. [Bronwyn Barry — bronwyn-barry-passive-house-bb] [Hans Breaux — hans-breaux-project-co-op] (within 0.01% of model) [Elrond Burrell — elrond-burrell-via-architecture] [Renee Relf — renee-relf-m-arch-cphc] [Katrina Belle — katrina-belle-maine-passive-house] [Valentine Gomez — valentine-gomez-gomex-engineering].

**8. Education and giving value first IS the marketing.**
Teach the "why" and "how," give free resources, and business follows ("jab, jab, right hook"). Position as a knowledge resource, not a vendor. [Elrond Burrell — elrond-burrell-via-architecture] [Aaron Waldt — aaron-waldt-475-high-performance-building-supply] [Enrico Bonilauri — enrico-bonilauri-emu-passive] [Beth Eckenrode — beth-eckenrode-auros-group] [Steve Hessler — steve-hessler-new-energy-works] [Lindsey Love — lindsey-love-regenerative-building-solutions].

**9. Stack multiple co-benefits so no single objection kills the sale. HIGH CONSENSUS.**
Justify a measure with many points of value (comfort + quiet + health + durability + resilience + energy); the "value stack" lets each listener pick the benefit they already care about. [Brian Pearson — brian-pearson-studio-pear] [Jacob Deva Racusin — jacob-deva-racusin-new-frameworks] [Lloyd Alter — lloyd-alter-carbon-upfront] [Alexander Gard-Murray — alexander-gard-murray-passive-house-massachusetts].

**10. Position third-party certification as de-risking / durability insurance — not an eco-badge. HIGH CONSENSUS.**
Certification catches costly defects a code inspector misses and de-risks the project for owners, developers, and institutions. [Josh Salinger — josh-salinger-birdsmouth-design-build] ("a heck of an insurance policy") [Beth Campbell — beth-campbell-passive-house-massachusetts] [Aaron Waldt — aaron-waldt-475-high-performance-building-supply] [Rachel Rennard & Mike Bratina — rachel-rennard-mike-bratina-clay-brook-passive-house] [Mariana Pickering — mariana-pickering-b-public-prefab] [Shefali Sanghvi — shefali-sanghvi-dattner-architects].

## 1. Language & Framing — words to use and avoid

### Words and frames that WORK
- **Comfort, healthy, quiet, durable, resilient, safe, fresh/filtered air, no bugs, no dust, no mold.** The universal benefit vocabulary. [Lloyd Alter — lloyd-alter-carbon-upfront] [Beth Campbell — beth-campbell-passive-house-massachusetts] [Zack Semke — zack-semke-passive-house-accelerator] [Enrico Bonilauri — enrico-bonilauri-emu-passive] [Josh Salinger — josh-salinger-birdsmouth-design-build] HIGH CONSENSUS.
- **"Return on health," not return on investment** — health is the #1 driver; retrofit ROI is hard to defend. [Adam White — adam-white-intelligent-membranes].
- **"Smoke tight" / "radon tight"** — reframe airtightness as protection people can visualize. [Adam White — adam-white-intelligent-membranes] [Bronwyn Barry — bronwyn-barry-passive-house-bb].
- **"Good buildings," not "green buildings"** — nobody refuses a good building; "green" connotes expensive. [Valentine Gomez — valentine-gomez-gomex-engineering].
- **"Best practices / quality / building science," not a German energy standard.** [Josh Salinger — josh-salinger-birdsmouth-design-build] [Mark Attard — mark-attard-point-6].
- **"Passive house isn't a premium, it's a learning curve."** A reusable cost-objection rebuttal. [Beth Eckenrode — beth-eckenrode-auros-group].
- **Affordability as the master narrative** — "the cheapest time to make a building work well is at the beginning." [Alexander Gard-Murray — alexander-gard-murray-passive-house-massachusetts].
- **"De-risk your project."** [Josh Salinger — josh-salinger-birdsmouth-design-build] [Michael Quast — michael-quast-passive-house-canada].
- **"Comfort and security are the same thing"** (etymology + felt) — systems that deliver comfort deliver safety in heat waves/outages. [Lloyd Alter — lloyd-alter-carbon-upfront] [Kristof Irwin — kristof-irwin-positive-energy].
- **Memorable, alliterative soundbites:** "boxy but beautiful," the "three Ts" (Targets, Tool, Training). [Bronwyn Barry — bronwyn-barry-passive-house-bb]; "car-tool housing" [Martín Comas — martin-comas-arquitectura-regenerativa]; "easy button" [Hans Breaux — hans-breaux-project-co-op]; "the red door of truth" (blower door) [Valentine Gomez — valentine-gomez-gomex-engineering].
- **Translate metrics into felt outcomes** — U-value → interior surface temperature → "you're gonna be cold vs. not cold"; "walk around in your underwear and feel comfortable." [Enrico Bonilauri — enrico-bonilauri-emu-passive] [Mark Attard — mark-attard-point-6].
- **Accessible analogies:** thermos / Yeti + "you can't breathe in a thermos" (envelope + HRV) [Alexander Gard-Murray — alexander-gard-murray-passive-house-massachusetts] [Beth Campbell — beth-campbell-passive-house-massachusetts]; pullover/vest (insulation slows heat loss, doesn't make heat) [Wolfgang Feist — dr-wolfgang-feist-passive-house-institute]; puffy coat vs. thin jacket / cooler [Katrina Belle — katrina-belle-maine-passive-house]; "thermal battery" [Lloyd Alter — lloyd-alter-carbon-upfront]; house-as-airbag [Kristof Irwin — kristof-irwin-positive-energy]; "house is a machine, calibrate it" [Lindsey Love — lindsey-love-regenerative-building-solutions].
- **"Above code, not to code" / "code is the floor not the ceiling."** [Rachel Rennard & Mike Bratina — rachel-rennard-mike-bratina-clay-brook-passive-house] [Hans Breaux — hans-breaux-project-co-op] [Josh Salinger — josh-salinger-birdsmouth-design-build].
- **"This is just better" / not a "hair shirt."** PH is not sacrifice or guilt. [Graham Irwin — graham-irwin-essential-habitat-architecture].
- **Spell it "Passivhaus" (one German word)** to mark it as a defined standard and dodge "just passive / just houses." [Elrond Burrell — elrond-burrell-via-architecture] [Lloyd Alter — lloyd-alter-carbon-upfront] [Kristof Irwin — kristof-irwin-positive-energy].
- **"Upfront carbon," not "embodied carbon"** — less confusing. [Lloyd Alter — lloyd-alter-carbon-upfront] [Bronwyn Barry — bronwyn-barry-passive-house-bb].
- **Window = thermal bridge, not a hole to fill** — "it's where you cut all your thermal bridge." [Laurence Hamel Dorais — laurence-hamel-dorais-nzp-fenestration].

### Words and frames that FAIL
- **Leading with energy savings / kWh / bills** — energy is cheap (in North America) and "doesn't drive anyone." [Lloyd Alter — lloyd-alter-carbon-upfront] [Alexander Gard-Murray — alexander-gard-murray-passive-house-massachusetts] HIGH CONSENSUS.
- **"Airtight" as an opener** — triggers "am I gonna die? / houses need to breathe." Reframe as "controlling the breathing" via HRV. [Zack Semke — zack-semke-passive-house-accelerator] [Rachel Rennard & Mike Bratina — rachel-rennard-mike-bratina-clay-brook-passive-house].
- **Jargon dumps (ACH50, WUFI, hygrothermal, latent recovery, BTUs, U-values)** — "a complete non-starter"; one client said it "feels a little Germanic." [Zack Semke — zack-semke-passive-house-accelerator] [Josh Salinger — josh-salinger-birdsmouth-design-build] [Kristof Irwin — kristof-irwin-positive-energy] HIGH CONSENSUS.
- **"Low embodied carbon" as an opener** — "we've lost it in the first three words." [Jacob Deva Racusin — jacob-deva-racusin-new-frameworks] [Steve Hessler — steve-hessler-new-energy-works].
- **"Green building"** — feels dated and signals "expensive." [Josh Salinger — josh-salinger-birdsmouth-design-build] [Valentine Gomez — valentine-gomez-gomex-engineering].
- **The "thermos" metaphor used carelessly** — a builder weaponized it as "you're making a thermos, it's silly." [Brian Pearson — brian-pearson-studio-pear].
- **Pure fear tactics** — position air quality as wellness/education, not a scare. [Alexis Jarvis — alexis-jarvis-sidecar-pr].
- **Simple-payback math** — implies savings stop at year six; ignores compounding savings, rising rates, real 13-year ownership. [Alexander Gard-Murray — alexander-gard-murray-passive-house-massachusetts] [Valentine Gomez — valentine-gomez-gomex-engineering] [Laurence Hamel Dorais — laurence-hamel-dorais-nzp-fenestration].
- **"Boring, literal" branding** (the "Surface Pro" problem); B2B buyers are still humans who want fun/novelty. [Adam White — adam-white-intelligent-membranes].

## 4. Scope of Work, Services & Business Model

- **Position as a "knowledge resource," not a vendor** — "a knowledge resource company that sells tape." Education is the differentiator. [Aaron Waldt — aaron-waldt-475-high-performance-building-supply] [Beth Eckenrode — beth-eckenrode-auros-group].
- **Productize and name your front-end consulting in plain language** — e.g. "building enclosure technical integration" (four words). Sell it as a distinct service before the modeling. [Jeremy Clarke — jeremy-clarke-simple-life-homes] [Mark Attard — mark-attard-point-6].

## 5. Trust, Proof & Social Proof

- **Measured-vs-modeled performance is the most powerful proof** — "within 0.01% of the energy model." Beats certification logos for client confidence. [Hans Breaux — hans-breaux-project-co-op] [Bronwyn Barry — bronwyn-barry-passive-house-bb] [Elrond Burrell — elrond-burrell-via-architecture] [Valentine Gomez — valentine-gomez-gomex-engineering].
- **Tell the "performance gap" story** — most buildings are unverified prototypes; PH delivers predicted ≈ actual certainty. [Elrond Burrell — elrond-burrell-via-architecture].
- **Real, named, real-time energy-bill data** — a roomful reacting "this is unreal" to a consultant's own PH bill. [Renee Relf — renee-relf-m-arch-cphc] [Katrina Belle — katrina-belle-maine-passive-house].
- **Hard cost stats to kill the premium myth** — MassCEC's 2–3% large-multifamily premium; $450/sf is the going custom rate "passive or not"; operating costs 30–50% of code-built. [Alexander Gard-Murray — alexander-gard-murray-passive-house-massachusetts] [Katrina Belle — katrina-belle-maine-passive-house].
- **Concrete, testable resilience promises** — "stays 70°F for three days in winter without power." [Kristof Irwin — kristof-irwin-positive-energy] [Valentine Gomez — valentine-gomez-gomex-engineering] (held >17.9°C, no heat, May–Nov).
- **Third-party certification as a quality/durability/warranty signal** — even floating a 10-year warranty on certified homes; real failure cases of uncertified "passive-ish" buildings (>$100k rot repairs) justify the rigor. [Josh Salinger — josh-salinger-birdsmouth-design-build] [Beth Campbell — beth-campbell-passive-house-massachusetts] [Mariana Pickering — mariana-pickering-b-public-prefab].
- **Display trust seals/certifications** (PHI, Phius, Declare/Red List, EPD, ASTM/CanULC) as marketing credibility, not technical footnotes. [Aaron Waldt — aaron-waldt-475-high-performance-building-supply].
- **Flagship proof points** — world's-largest-PH-office chose PH voluntarily; affordable housing that "pencils" removes all excuses. [Alexander Gard-Murray — alexander-gard-murray-passive-house-massachusetts] [Alexis Jarvis — alexis-jarvis-sidecar-pr].
- **Have the GC/contractor in the room confirming feasibility and cost** — "the guys on the field," not "people dressed in black in an office." [Shefali Sanghvi — shefali-sanghvi-dattner-architects].
- **Portfolio as de-risking proof** — "we've done this a lot before." [Shefali Sanghvi — shefali-sanghvi-dattner-architects] [Tessa Bradley — tessa-bradley-artisans-group] [John Loercher — john-loercher-northeast-projects] (a "50 projects" retrospective campaign).
- **Honesty builds credibility** — "tell the bad stuff first," share the good *and* the ugly, refuse to claim "no premium" when it depends. [Adam White — adam-white-intelligent-membranes] [Beth Campbell — beth-campbell-passive-house-massachusetts] [Tanya Savas — tanya-savas-collective-carpentry].
- **Warm, personable team-bio pages** drove strong "I connected with your team" reactions. [Kristof Irwin — kristof-irwin-positive-energy].
- **Let big third-party voices de-risk products** — Matt Risinger, NS Builders, building code, PH associations normalize a product once they discuss it. [Aaron Waldt — aaron-waldt-475-high-performance-building-supply].

## 6. What Works (tactics with field evidence)

- **Going benefit-by-benefit until the client wants them all, then soft-dropping "…kind of like a passive house"** (Michael Ingui's technique). [Zack Semke — zack-semke-passive-house-accelerator].
- **"Stealth Passive House"** — don't name it; just deliver the airtight, super-insulated, healthy house. Works until you must justify the cost premium. [Tanya Savas — tanya-savas-collective-carpentry] [Lloyd Alter — lloyd-alter-carbon-upfront] (Ingui often doesn't tell clients).
- **Reframing technical value into the client's own language closes sales** — the Gunnison window sale closed once U-value became "you're gonna be not cold." [Enrico Bonilauri — enrico-bonilauri-emu-passive].
- **Selling "no bugs" and "quiet"** — Ingui says "no bugs" is "one of the things people love more than anything." Quiet on a busy street converts. [Lloyd Alter — lloyd-alter-carbon-upfront].
- **Asking-first conversational approach** — "what do you dislike about your building?" — almost everyone names a pain PH solves. [Alexander Gard-Murray — alexander-gard-murray-passive-house-massachusetts] [Beth Campbell — beth-campbell-passive-house-massachusetts].
- **Mining recurring site/meeting questions to drive content and trainings.** [Aaron Waldt — aaron-waldt-475-high-performance-building-supply].
- **Blower-door + fog-machine theater** gives builders a "thrill" and tangible proof. [Valentine Gomez — valentine-gomez-gomex-engineering] [Alexis Jarvis — alexis-jarvis-sidecar-pr].
- **News-jacking timely events** (move in right before the Texas grid freeze → resilience press). [Trey Farmer — trey-farmer-forge-craft-architecture-design].
- **Utility/state reimbursement** (Xcel reimburses 90% of training fees) secured via relationship-building, not cold asks. [Enrico Bonilauri — enrico-bonilauri-emu-passive].
- **Cold-calling housing-finance agencies** to get PH added as a scoring criterion in competitive point systems. [Hans Breaux — hans-breaux-project-co-op].
- **Stacking incentives** (Mass Save, LIHTC points, voluntary stretch codes). [Alexander Gard-Murray — alexander-gard-murray-passive-house-massachusetts] [Shefali Sanghvi — shefali-sanghvi-dattner-architects].
- **Designing PH in from the start so it can't be value-engineered out.** [Alexis Jarvis — alexis-jarvis-sidecar-pr] [Elrond Burrell — elrond-burrell-via-architecture].
- **Curiosity/contrarian headlines** — "10 Things I Hate About Passive House" was the highest-traffic post. [Elrond Burrell — elrond-burrell-via-architecture].
- **Just committing publicly and "saying the words"** generated inbound work — "there was no marketing wizardry." [Lorrie Rand — lorrie-rand-habit-studio].
- **Doing the unglamorous footwork** (extracting Manual J/S, ERI/HERS from PHPP for permitting) is "attractive to architects." [Mark Attard — mark-attard-point-6].

## 7. What Fails / Pitfalls

- **Leading with logic/specs/energy to a general audience** — assumes everyone shares the geek's passion. [Graham Irwin — graham-irwin-essential-habitat-architecture] [Josh Salinger — josh-salinger-birdsmouth-design-build] HIGH CONSENSUS.
- **Trying to convince an uninterested person from scratch** — "I have never convinced someone who wasn't already interested." Target the seed, don't plant it cold. [Graham Irwin — graham-irwin-essential-habitat-architecture] [Tanya Savas — tanya-savas-collective-carpentry] [Jacob Deva Racusin — jacob-deva-racusin-new-frameworks].
- **Relying on a single benefit** — easy for a client to dismiss as "not applicable to me." [Brian Pearson — brian-pearson-studio-pear].
- **Pushing a measure (exterior insulation) without builder buy-in** — builder was "furious." [Brian Pearson — brian-pearson-studio-pear].
- **Promoting a single "Passive House look"** — alienates clients; everyone pictures something different (tiny windows, boxy, foam). [Josh Salinger — josh-salinger-birdsmouth-design-build] [Mariana Pickering — mariana-pickering-b-public-prefab] [Shefali Sanghvi — shefali-sanghvi-dattner-architects].
- **"Passive-ish" building without certification or real best practices** — lower drying capacity means small leaks become big failures. [Josh Salinger — josh-salinger-birdsmouth-design-build] [Jeremy Clarke — jeremy-clarke-simple-life-homes].
- **Getting involved too late (post-permit)** — budget locked, levers gone. [Mark Attard — mark-attard-point-6] [Jeremy Clarke — jeremy-clarke-simple-life-homes].
- **SEO that pulls wrong-fit traffic** — searchers for "modular/prefab/passive solar" want the cheapest option and rarely convert. [Tanya Savas — tanya-savas-collective-carpentry].
- **Telling dramatic construction "war stories"** — undermines confidence; "share the successes." [Lorrie Rand — lorrie-rand-habit-studio].
- **Purism / gatekeeping** — the load-bearing-straw purists "lost patience" and isolated people. [Michael Quast — michael-quast-passive-house-canada].
- **Expecting blogging/"plain English" to reach the general public** — the audience self-selected to architects/consultants. [Elrond Burrell — elrond-burrell-via-architecture].
- **Over-engineering to hit the standard** (3-ft sub-slab insulation) instead of simplifying form. [Lloyd Alter — lloyd-alter-carbon-upfront].
- **Pitching design media without great photography** — editors decide on pictures regardless of story. [Alexis Jarvis — alexis-jarvis-sidecar-pr].
- **Selling "design certified" as an end in itself** — "do people live in the drawing?" [Enrico Bonilauri — enrico-bonilauri-emu-passive].
- **Procurement structure can kill prefab/integration value** — design-bid-build (CCDC 2) and lowest-bid exclude early involvement. [Jeremy Clarke — jeremy-clarke-simple-life-homes].
- **Heavy 3D modeling can read as "showing off / wasting our time"** to some clients. [Steve Hessler — steve-hessler-new-energy-works].
- **VC "disruptor" plays** that try to fix slow-moving construction fast (Katerra). [Mariana Pickering — mariana-pickering-b-public-prefab].
- **Most failures are expectation-alignment failures, not technical failures** — especially treating windows as a late finish rather than an early make-or-break driver. [Mariana Pickering — mariana-pickering-b-public-prefab].

## 8. Client Psychology — likes, dislikes, objections

### What clients genuinely like
- **Felt comfort** — no cold spots, even temperatures, warm by the window in a snowstorm, barefoot in winter, "walk around in your underwear." [Beth Campbell — beth-campbell-passive-house-massachusetts] [Tessa Bradley — tessa-bradley-artisans-group] [Trey Farmer — trey-farmer-forge-craft-architecture-design] [Mark Attard — mark-attard-point-6] HIGH CONSENSUS.
- **Health / indoor air quality** — fresh filtered air, fewer allergy/eczema flares, "feel safe in your space" (decisive for health-sensitive families). [John Loercher — john-loercher-northeast-projects] [Lorrie Rand — lorrie-rand-habit-studio] [Beth Campbell — beth-campbell-passive-house-massachusetts] HIGH CONSENSUS.
- **Quiet, no bugs, no dust.** [Lloyd Alter — lloyd-alter-carbon-upfront] [Zack Semke — zack-semke-passive-house-accelerator].
- **Resilience / "peace of mind, comfortable even with no power for a week."** [Lorrie Rand — lorrie-rand-habit-studio] [Kristof Irwin — kristof-irwin-positive-energy] [Katrina Belle — katrina-belle-maine-passive-house].
- **Durability / "forever home" / "hundred-year building"** and lower maintenance / fewer service calls (~90% of HVAC calls are no-heat emergencies). [Brian Pearson — brian-pearson-studio-pear] [Lorrie Rand — lorrie-rand-habit-studio].
- **Beautiful design** — many clients arrive purely for aesthetics, indifferent to the method. "There is no substitute for great design." [Alexis Jarvis — alexis-jarvis-sidecar-pr] [Tessa Bradley — tessa-bradley-artisans-group] [Graham Irwin — graham-irwin-essential-habitat-architecture].
- **Data/dashboards** for the "spreadsheet" / tech-nerd segment. [Bronwyn Barry — bronwyn-barry-passive-house-bb] [Rachel Rennard & Mike Bratina — rachel-rennard-mike-bratina-clay-brook-passive-house].
- **Luxury / status / "conspicuous conservation"** for affluent clients. [Lloyd Alter — lloyd-alter-carbon-upfront].
- **Visible cool-factor / flag-flying** (the Prius effect). [Graham Irwin — graham-irwin-essential-habitat-architecture].
- **Predictable budgets, transparent billing, de-risked process.** [Katrina Belle — katrina-belle-maine-passive-house] [Jeremy Clarke — jeremy-clarke-simple-life-homes].
- **Builders/trades: pride of craft** — measurable proof their work is superior; PH as "a vehicle to communicate quality of craft." [Mariana Pickering — mariana-pickering-b-public-prefab] [Valentine Gomez — valentine-gomez-gomex-engineering] [Aaron Waldt — aaron-waldt-475-high-performance-building-supply].

### What clients dislike / objections (and counters)
- **Cost / "it doesn't pencil" / "costs 10–20% more"** — THE dominant objection. Counter: 2–3% real premium, lifetime/compounding savings, "evaporates against day-one operating savings," "rounding error on the interest rate." [Alexander Gard-Murray — alexander-gard-murray-passive-house-massachusetts] [Katrina Belle — katrina-belle-maine-passive-house] [Trey Farmer — trey-farmer-forge-craft-architecture-design] [Hans Breaux — hans-breaux-project-co-op] HIGH CONSENSUS.
- **"Sealed box / can't open windows / houses need to breathe."** Counter: "if you want birdsong, open the window — it's only sealed when you want it." [Graham Irwin — graham-irwin-essential-habitat-architecture] [Katrina Belle — katrina-belle-maine-passive-house] [Rachel Rennard & Mike Bratina — rachel-rennard-mike-bratina-clay-brook-passive-house].
- **"Airtight = I'll suffocate."** Counter: reframe as "controlling the breathing" via HRV/fresh air. [Zack Semke — zack-semke-passive-house-accelerator] [Rachel Rennard & Mike Bratina — rachel-rennard-mike-bratina-clay-brook-passive-house].
- **"PH is for cold European climates."** Counter: "smoke tight," cooling/summer-comfort data, built examples in warm climates. [Bronwyn Barry — bronwyn-barry-passive-house-bb] [Wolfgang Feist — dr-wolfgang-feist-passive-house-institute].
- **"PH means ugly boxes with tiny windows."** Counter: gallery of beautiful, well-glazed, aesthetically diverse certified buildings. [Shefali Sanghvi — shefali-sanghvi-dattner-architects] [Josh Salinger — josh-salinger-birdsmouth-design-build] [Mariana Pickering — mariana-pickering-b-public-prefab].
- **"Elitist / only for the rich."** Counter: affordable/retrofit proof; "for normal people, not only rich people." [Naomi Beal — naomi-beal-passivhausmaine] [Martín Comas — martin-comas-arquitectura-regenerativa] [Wolfgang Feist — dr-wolfgang-feist-passive-house-institute].
- **"Prefab/PH is cheaper."** Counter: it's not — you're buying streamlining and quality assurance, not a discount. [Mariana Pickering — mariana-pickering-b-public-prefab] [Tanya Savas — tanya-savas-collective-carpentry].
- **Certification hassle/expense** — clients frequently abandon it. Address honestly (value vs. cost); sell targets not the plaque. [Lorrie Rand — lorrie-rand-habit-studio] [Tessa Bradley — tessa-bradley-artisans-group] [Jeremy Clarke — jeremy-clarke-simple-life-homes].
- **Invisible benefits are hard to value** — "can't take a picture of good air quality." Counter: make it visible. [Alexis Jarvis — alexis-jarvis-sidecar-pr].
- **"Gimme the granite, not the insulation"** — clients prefer visible finishes over invisible performance. [Lloyd Alter — lloyd-alter-carbon-upfront] [Michael Quast — michael-quast-passive-house-canada].
- **Trusting the tradesperson over the consultant** — "my heating contractor says it won't work." [Lloyd Alter — lloyd-alter-carbon-upfront] [Brian Pearson — brian-pearson-studio-pear].
- **Builder/trade resistance to new methods** — fear of cost, schedule, callbacks; "the way I was always taught." Counter with the why + blower-door proof + fewer callbacks. [Brian Pearson — brian-pearson-studio-pear] [Valentine Gomez — valentine-gomez-gomex-engineering] [Michael Quast — michael-quast-passive-house-canada].

- **The "house as a disposable consumer product" mindset** suppresses demand for durable performance. [Michael Quast — michael-quast-passive-house-canada].

## Notable tensions / disagreements

1. **Reveal the standard, or never name it?** Most say lead with benefits then soft-reveal "Passive House." But some go further to full "stealth PH" — never naming it [Tanya Savas — tanya-savas-collective-carpentry] [Lloyd Alter — lloyd-alter-carbon-upfront] (Ingui) — while Wolfgang Feist insists PH is a clear, defined *end goal* worth naming and that built examples defeat objections [Wolfgang Feist — dr-wolfgang-feist-passive-house-institute], and Bronwyn Barry sells PH precisely *as* the defined target the industry lacks [Bronwyn Barry — bronwyn-barry-passive-house-bb].

2. **Is there a cost premium at all?** Feist and Hans Breaux argue there's essentially no premium with a competent team [Wolfgang Feist — dr-wolfgang-feist-passive-house-institute] [Hans Breaux — hans-breaux-project-co-op]; Beth Campbell explicitly refuses to claim "no upfront cost" because "it depends completely on the team" and sees overpromising as a credibility risk [Beth Campbell — beth-campbell-passive-house-massachusetts]; Steve Hessler and Tanya Savas say plainly the savings "don't pencil for the premium" and you must be honest about it [Steve Hessler — steve-hessler-new-energy-works] [Tanya Savas — tanya-savas-collective-carpentry].

3. **Comfort/emotion vs. money/logic as the lead.** The dominant view is comfort-first. But for large/commercial/portfolio owners, Beth Eckenrode insists you lead with modeled ROI and "comfort/emotion is the small-residential pitch" [Beth Eckenrode — beth-eckenrode-auros-group]; Lindsey Love and Katrina Belle argue "the strongest arguments are always the economical ones" / the housing-crisis cost angle, not comfort [Lindsey Love — lindsey-love-regenerative-building-solutions] [Katrina Belle — katrina-belle-maine-passive-house].

4. **Energy efficiency: by-product or sometimes harmful?** Most treat energy as the silent by-product of comfort. Jacob Racusin goes further — leading with energy efficiency *in isolation* can cause unintended durability/IAQ harm (per Robert Bean) [Jacob Deva Racusin — jacob-deva-racusin-new-frameworks]. Lloyd Alter even cites a Quebec analysis where code-minimum beat PH on lifetime carbon on a clean grid [Lloyd Alter — lloyd-alter-carbon-upfront].

5. **Certification: essential de-risking or low-value badge?** Josh Salinger, Beth Campbell, and the Clay Brook owners champion certification as insurance/QA [Josh Salinger — josh-salinger-birdsmouth-design-build] [Beth Campbell — beth-campbell-passive-house-massachusetts]; Lorrie Rand (no certified projects), Tessa Bradley (1-in-4 to 1-in-6), and Jeremy Clarke find most clients don't want it and abandon it [Lorrie Rand — lorrie-rand-habit-studio] [Tessa Bradley — tessa-bradley-artisans-group] [Jeremy Clarke — jeremy-clarke-simple-life-homes].

6. **Carbon/materials: lead, mention, or hide?** Natural-materials advocates weave bio-based/embodied-carbon into the core story [Jacob Deva Racusin — jacob-deva-racusin-new-frameworks] [Valentine Gomez — valentine-gomez-gomex-engineering] [Martín Comas — martin-comas-arquitectura-regenerativa]; Steve Hessler and Jacob both warn that *leading* with carbon is "preaching to the choir" and loses the market [Steve Hessler — steve-hessler-new-energy-works].

7. **3D modeling / technical depth: differentiator or showing off?** Steve Hessler wins architects with dramatic 3D models [Steve Hessler — steve-hessler-new-energy-works]; he and Mark Attard both note some clients read deep modeling as time-wasting [Mark Attard — mark-attard-point-6].
