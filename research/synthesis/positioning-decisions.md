## D1 — Audience: B2B (design professionals), not direct-to-homeowner — **DECIDED 2026-06-29**

**Decision:** BLDGTYP is a **B2B professional-services firm**. ~99% of contracts are with **architects and developers**; they are the buyers. We do **not** market directly to homeowners.

**Nuance — the "ride-along" homeowner:** A homeowner may land on the site because their architect referred them ("you should hire these folks to consult"). The site must *work* for that owner — give them confidence to say yes — but they are **not a lead-gen target**. The primary voice stays **peer-to-peer professional credibility**, pitched at the design professional's level.

**Implications for the homepage (revises the gap-analysis):**

- ❌ **Do NOT** add a "Homeowners" audience column or build a consumer comfort/health funnel as the primary voice. (This overrides the corpus's homeowner-emotional-benefit advice, which targets firms that sell *to* owners.)
- ✅ **DO** center the positioning the corpus calls "architect-first / add-on team member / outside pair of eyes" — offload the modeling architects don't want, de-risk *their* project, make *them* look good to *their* client. [mark-attard-point-6][elrond-burrell-via-architecture]
- ✅ **DO** lead with **professional competence + proof**: portfolio / "we've done this a lot" de-risking, measured-vs-modeled results, named projects, credentials. [shefali-sanghvi-dattner-architects][hans-breaux-project-co-op]
- ✅ **DO** keep a **thin layer of plain-language reassurance** so a sent-over, non-expert owner isn't alienated and can confidently endorse hiring us — a light touch of comfort/health/quiet/"what this means for you," *secondary* to the professional voice. This is the one place the "convince the other decision-maker" rule still applies. [graham-irwin-essential-habitat-architecture]
- ✅ **DO** keep "Passive House" named (SEO + pro credibility); still demote it from the *emotional* hook to the *method* where benefits are described.
- The felt-benefit language (comfort/health/quiet/resilience) is **not deleted** — it's repositioned as *how we describe outcomes to the team and the owner-as-reader*, not as a consumer headline.

**Still-relevant business-model items** (audience-neutral, unaffected by D1): productize/elevate the front-end assessment, the M&V/monitoring tier, PH-Tools-as-moat, the education/content engine, "sell targets not the plaque," right-sizing mechanicals as a value-add.
